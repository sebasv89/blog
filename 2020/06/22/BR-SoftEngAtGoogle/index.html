Software Engineering at Google

My Writings
2020-06-22
BookReview

My Rating: 4/5

What I like about the book

There are two facts you can’t deny about google: They face complex challenges, at scale, and they usually have an interesting way of running their business. I have recently read at least 5 books looking for insight on software engineering at scale, and this latest book, “Software Engineering at Google”, is probably one of my favorites.

If you are familiar with other well known titles such as TODO, TODO, AND TODO, you will find the format familiar: A collection of essays, that you can read in any order and where you probably will find some chapters more interesting than others. You may think with such a generic title that this book is all about repeating software engineering concepts. I was a bit hesitant to read this book at the beginning. However, I found it really insightful to know Google perspectives on Leadership, Testing, Running infrastructure, Managing communication and more.

My 3 takeaways

  1. I loved all concepts about leading at scale: “ask yourself a different critical question: What can I do that nobody else on my team can do?” summarizes the essence of these subset of chapters. Really good stuff about delegating, empowering, guiding a high level vision, and so on.
  2. It’s all about tradeoffs: Complex decisions always involve favoring some qualities and relegating other qualities. Knowing your tradeoffs and seeing the holistic view is key.
  3. The “Beyonce rule” for testing: If you care about a feature, you need to add a test to protect it. Developers own quality, and is not a fault of an infrastructure/testing team if your feature is not working

I recommend this book for:

Developers getting into the tech leadership world, as well as leaders working on a tech organization facing scaling issues. Google is always a must-know reference, and this book gets added as one of my favorites.
